About me:
I got my Bachelors in Chemical Engineering from UC Berkeley in 2017. In undergraduate, I had experience interning in a sustainability non-profit where I negotiated with local businesses to decrease their food packaging waste by offering discounts for people with reusable containers. Additionally, I researched indoor air quality and designed a bioelectrical stimulation reactor to investigate the cell metabolisms of Anaerobic Ammonia Oxidizing Organisms (Anammox). I am currently a Environmental Engineering PhD student in Harvard University. I researched in Daniel J. Jacob's lab, collaborating with NASA to develop and test the GEOS-5 Earth System model. I was investigating how vertical resolution can improve the the simulation of transpacific pollution plumes. I recently decided that I wanted to apply my interests in biology to my current background in modeling and mathematics. I hope to use control theory, statistics and neural networks to elucidate gene expression, protein production and help design novel methods to treat human illnesses.
